The Masks We Wear

July 17, 2023 Stigma Personal

Hiding my bipolar disorder at work feels necessary but exhausting. This is how I navigate professional spaces while managing an invisible illness, and the complex emotions that come with wearing these masks.

The Professional Mask

In the workplace, I've become an expert at maintaining a facade of constant stability. I schedule important meetings during my typically stable hours, take "bathroom breaks" when I need a moment to regulate my emotions, and have perfected the art of appearing calm even when my thoughts are racing.

The Cost of Hiding

This constant performance takes its toll. The energy spent maintaining this professional mask often leaves me exhausted by the end of the day. It's a hidden tax on my mental resources that my colleagues never see.

When the Mask Slips

There have been moments when maintaining the facade becomes impossible. A missed deadline during a depressive episode, or an overly enthusiastic presentation during a hypomanic phase. These moments of truth force me to navigate difficult conversations about my capabilities and reliability.

"Sometimes the bravest thing we can do is let the mask slip, just a little, to show others they're not alone."

Finding Safe Spaces

I've learned to create small pockets of authenticity in my workday. A trusted colleague who knows my situation, a quiet corner for meditation during lunch breaks, or working from home when I need to manage my symptoms more openly.

The Dream of Authenticity

I dream of a workplace culture where mental health challenges can be discussed as openly as physical health issues. Where taking a mental health day doesn't require elaborate excuses, and where diversity includes neurodiversity.

Until then, I'll continue to navigate this delicate balance between authenticity and professionalism, hoping that by sharing my story, I can contribute to creating more understanding and accepting workplaces for everyone managing invisible illnesses.
