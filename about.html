About Me

The person behind the blog and the journey that led me here.

My Story

Hi, I'm Emma. I'm 27 years old, a writer, a daughter, a friend, and as of eight months ago, someone living with bipolar disorder type II. My diagnosis came after years of emotional extremes that I couldn't quite explain—periods of intense productivity and creativity followed by debilitating depression that left me unable to get out of bed.

For years, I thought I was just "moody" or "sensitive." I blamed myself for not being able to maintain consistent energy or motivation. My highs were never quite manic enough to raise alarm bells, but my lows were devastating. When I finally received my diagnosis, it was like someone had handed me a map to a territory I'd been navigating blindfolded.

I started this blog as a way to process my own experiences, but it's grown into so much more. It's become a platform to address the stigma surrounding mental illness, particularly bipolar disorder, and to connect with others who may be feeling isolated in their struggles.

"Bipolar disorder has taught me that I can find beauty in the most unexpected places—even in the chaos of my own mind."

The title "Beauty is in the Eye of the Bipolar" came to me during a hypomanic episode when colors seemed more vibrant, ideas flowed freely, and the world felt almost painfully beautiful. I've since learned to recognize these episodes as part of my condition, but I haven't let go of the fundamental truth that emerged: our unique perspectives, even those shaped by mental illness, can reveal beauty that others might miss.

What You'll Find Here

This blog is a mixture of personal stories, coping strategies that have worked for me, reflections on living with an invisible illness, and resources that have helped me along the way. I write about:

  • The day-to-day reality of managing bipolar disorder
  • The impact on relationships, work, and self-image
  • Tools and techniques for mood tracking and symptom management
  • Breaking down stigma and misconceptions
  • Finding purpose and meaning despite—and sometimes because of—mental illness

I'm not a mental health professional, just someone sharing her experience. What works for me might not work for everyone, and I always encourage readers to seek professional help for their own mental health challenges.

Beyond the Blog

When I'm not writing, you might find me hiking with my rescue dog, Charlie, experimenting with new recipes (with mixed results), or volunteering at a local literacy program. I believe in the healing power of community and creative expression, and I try to incorporate both into my management plan for bipolar disorder.

Thank you for taking the time to visit this space. Whether you're here because you're facing similar challenges, supporting someone who is, or simply seeking to understand, I'm grateful for your presence. Feel free to reach out through the contact page if you have questions, suggestions, or just want to connect.
