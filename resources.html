Resources

A collection of helpful tools and support systems for anyone navigating life with bipolar disorder.

What You'll Find Here

This page contains resources that have been helpful on my journey with bipolar disorder. Please note that I am not a healthcare professional, and this information is not a substitute for professional medical advice. Always consult with qualified mental health providers for diagnosis, treatment, and support.

In Crisis? If you're experiencing thoughts of harming yourself or others, please call the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 988 or text HOME to 741741 to reach the Crisis Text Line. These services are available 24/7.

National Organizations

National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI)

The largest grassroots mental health organization in the US. NAMI provides advocacy, education, support, and public awareness programs.

Helpline: 1-800-950-NAMI (6264)
Website: www.nami.org

Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance (DBSA)

Provides hope, help, support, and education to improve the lives of people who have mood disorders, including online and in-person support groups.

Website: www.dbsalliance.org

International Bipolar Foundation

Works to improve understanding and treatment of bipolar disorder through research, education, and support.

Website: ibpf.org

Finding Treatment

Psychology Today Therapist Directory

Search for therapists, psychiatrists, treatment centers, and support groups in your area.

Website: www.psychologytoday.com/us/therapists

SAMHSA Treatment Locator

Find treatment facilities confidentially and anonymously through this service from the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration.

Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357)
Website: findtreatment.samhsa.gov

Sleep Hygiene Resources

Sleep disruption is both a trigger and symptom of bipolar episodes. These resources can help:

  • National Sleep Foundation: www.sleepfoundation.org
  • CBT-I Coach (app for cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia)
  • Blue light blocking glasses for evening screen time

How to Support Someone with Bipolar Disorder

  • Learn about the condition
  • Recognize triggers and warning signs
  • Encourage treatment adherence
  • Be patient and understanding
  • Take care of yourself too
